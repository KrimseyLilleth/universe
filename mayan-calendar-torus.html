The Calendar Round

Two sacred cycles, one woven body

Tzolk'in — 260 days, the long way around. Thirteen numbers turning against twenty day-names.

Haab' — 365 days, the short way around. Eighteen months of twenty days, plus the five-day Wayeb.

Each day the path advances one step on both cycles at once. It does not return to its starting point until 18,980 days have passed — fifty-two solar years.

260 = 13 × 20 (coprime — full cycle)
